# Section

A chapter divider: an oversized number, a short title, an optional "part two of four" hint. It is deliberately sparse — the empty space is what tells the audience a new chapter is starting. This is the one archetype where the title may be a topic label rather than a claim.

## Contract

- number: exactly one, as digits ("02")
- title: exactly one, ≤ 6 words
- progressHint: optional, ≤ 6 words ("Part two of four")

## Motion

The slide transition alone. No `data-step`, no reveal sequence — adding choreography to a divider cheapens the pause.

## When not to use

- Decks under eight slides: dividers start to outnumber the content.
- The break deserves a sentence people should remember: use `archetypes/statement` instead of a numbered label.
